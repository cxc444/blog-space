Narrative Drafting


Narrative 3 Paragraphs Rough Draft

Intro:

The dataset made available by the New Jersey Maritime Museum contains around 4500+ entries of shipwrecks off of the New Jersey Coast. This dataset includes shipwrecks from 1705-2013; 308 years of shipwrecks that include both purposeful sinking of ships for the artificial reef, and other ships that were wrecked due to various or unknown reasons. Due to the breadth of the dataset, we determined that it should be analyzed further to find a narrower focus. One particular area of interest were the five data points that detailed German U-Boats operating off the coast of New Jersey during WWI and WWII. These U-Boats torpedoed a number of ships contained in the database, as well as many others around the world. By analyzing attributes of the U-Boats and other related ships in the dataset, we can interpret some of the effects it may have had on maritime activity and American citizens. Additionally, we will look to source material to discover new insights into this topic that will inform us in ways that the pure data cannot.

Place in Literature:

Within the literature and information provided so far, it has been consistent with the documentation of the German U-Boats engaging in activities in the Atlantic Ocean and off of the New Jersey Coast. There is an abundant amount of information that is presented through books, articles, archives, photographs, military documents and more. In particular the US Navy released documents about German U-Boat activities in the Atlantic involving the United States and Canada. It documents U 151, one of the U-boats in the dataset, providing information on how long it took to travel from Germany to the Atlantic coast and vice versa. It covers a variety of topics, counter tactics, the effects on the maritime economic activities in the Atlantic Coast. This in depth set of information gives us access to understand why German U-Boats wanted to come to the East Coast and why it attacked the ships it did. The information the US Navy also includes the ships that the U 151 sunk that are also included in the dataset. Each ship is gone into detail on how it was sunk and how it impacted the activities of the US East Coast. The included photos help give context and visualization to the information that is being presented. It is important to note that U 151 was the only German U-Boat to be documented from World War I and was the first German U-Boat to breach US waters since the War of 1812.

Significance:

        The significance of this project helps us understand why a select group of foreign military ships were found off the coast of the United States East Coast. This is meaningful information as it examines one of the few times in recent history the United States has been attacked by another military force in the United States borders. The US is certainly familiar with naval combat when it comes to the military, but there are limited situations when civilian vessels are engaged. It is important to examine these data points and consider the significance in how a naval attack would affect the maritime activities today. It is also important to investigate the effectiveness of advancing technology when used by the military. WWII saw the frequent use of sonar in order to detect possible threats.
