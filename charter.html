Project Charter


Project Charter

Goals

Using the dataset available from the New Jersey Maritime Museum on shipwrecks off the New Jersey Coast, our goal is to determine if there was an influence in maritime activities off the New Jersey Coast due to the presence of German U-Boats. Additionally, we aim to analyze our data sets, literature, and subject matter expert interview in order to visualize our findings in a clear and definable way.

Research Questions

How did the German U-Boats influence maritime activity following their presence near the New Jersey coast during wartime periods?

  • Which groups of people or economic industries may have been affected by this as a result?

Methodological Approach

Our research began broadly with the initial focus on trade, economics, and the types of ships that wrecked. Due to a limited amount of research found the focus shifted to German U-Boats that sunk off the New Jersey Coast. The decision to change our focus helped unlock a plethora of information that was not found on the original idea for a focus. By changing the focus, it was easier to condense down the data on German U-Boat shipwrecks and ships sunk by German U-Boats.

Pertaining to the U-Boats, we will be looking to the periods before and after encounters with U-Boats near the New Jersey coast to determine any patterns there may be, as well as analyzing the types of ships and what cargo they were carrying (before, during, and after events). We feel that since there were two occurrences of ship hunting by U-Boats in this area (WWI/WWII), it would make sense to break up our visualizations and timelines based upon which time period we will be talking about. Additionally, we feel that it may be useful to have a data visualization section specifically focusing on commerce and goods on board these ships. In addition, there may be information gathered from the subject matter expert interview that enlightens us to another topic of interest within our current project plan.
